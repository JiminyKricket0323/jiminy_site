Game Analysis

Where the goats are

Tikvah, who lives with her goats and chickens in a remote homestead. It's a quiet and lonely existence where you point and click to pet goats, milk goats, fetch water from a well to water a plant, collect eggs from the hen house, pray, make cheese, doodle on the ground with a stick or simply do nothing at all. You'll soon find that Tikvah's tasks bring her joy, though; she whistles with charm as she slowly milks her goats. There is no clock in the game; as the days march on, you learn to tell the time by the changing colours of the minimalist landscape. The only human interaction in Where The Goats Are comes in the form of a trader who passes through, bringing letters from relatives. He trades hay and goats in exchange for cheese, but never stays for long. There is no instruction so the player needs to "learn as you go". One important thing in this game is anything that you can interact with, it highlights.

There is no clock in the game; as the days march on, you learn to tell the time by the changing colours of the minimalist landscape.

Most of the objects are things that Tikvah use to get her chores done or make goat cheese, which is her major duty that contributes to this game. For example, chores such as milking, collecting egges from chicken, watering her plant is her duty for each day. As walks around her farm, there are few of her footsteps of where she stepped on.

As she's the only person that player can see when they first start the game, it's very confusing to figure out what you have to do. Cheese Making

This is the how she makes her homemade goat cheese from the scratch. First she milk the goat when they're "NOT HUNGRY". When you click on goat, you have two choices of whether you want to give them loving pet or milk them. After collecting some goat milk, she pours all the fresh goat milk into the big pot and starts boiling them by clicking the boil button from the pot. After few minutes, goat cheese is done and ready to trade with the mail man.

When the mail man who is comes in the form of a trader who passes through, bringing letters from relatives. He trades hay and goats in exchange for cheese, but never stays for long.
